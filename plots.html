World Map

The world map shows the number of events (in this case hostage situation) across all the different countries. Furthermore, the user has the option to interact with the map and click a country to select it. The viualization will then filter the datapoints for the selected country and the two plots below the world map, the sunburst chart as well as the stream chart will adapt to only showing the datapoints corresponding to the selected country.







Sunburst

The sunburst shows the distribution among the different target types with the following main categories: Military/Police/Government, Civilians, Infrastructure, Terrorists, and Other. Each of them shows the distribution of different subtypes at the outer ring of the Sunburst. The user can select one of the main categories on the inner circle to filter the data to one target type and adjust both the world map as well as the stream chart to only show the datapoints corresponding to the target type.







Stacked Bar Chart

The stacked bar chart shows the use of several types of weapons used over time categorized in three groups: Explosives& Incendiary, Guns, and others. Explosives& Incendary are color coded in red, guns in blue and others in yellow. The stacking order is the following: Others, Guns, and then on top Explosives& Incendary. The user can browse over the chart to get more information about the different datapoints. The user can also click one of the weapon types to adjust the other two charts by weapon type as well.







Butterfly Chart

There are two butterfly plots on for the different regions and then one for the top 5 countries below. The left side of the butterfly represents the total number of perpetrator per million and indicates the proportion of foreign terrorists in yellow. The left side of the butterfly shows the proportion of homicidal terrorists compared to all where the homicidal proportion is colored red.







Scatterplot

There are two scatterplots. The right scatterplot shows the relationship between average hostages released by country and the average ransom difference (Demanded vs paid). The size of the datapoint indicates the number of events per million citizens. The left scatterplot shows the relationship between the average perp taken in custody by country versus average hostage rescue rate by country. The size of the datapoint is to read as in the first scatterplot.
